Tic Tac Toe

Tic Tac Toe is a widely popular game all over the world, dating as far back as played in Egypt 1300 BC. Two players take turns placing a marker on the square of a 3x3 grid until either one of the symbols are in a row, column or diagonal, or the grid is full with no moves remaining.

Requirements

Requirement Condition
Players 2
Required Pencil, paper
Genre Strategy, Turn-based
Time to play about 1 minute
Objective Winners place their symbol in a pattern of row, column, or diagonal on the board.
Tie possible Yes

Instructions

  1. Players each choose a symbol to represent their moves. Traditional symbols are called nought (O) and crosses (X), or simply O's and X's.
  2. Create a 3x3 grid. It can be drawn if not readily available. The grid should have 9 points, with 2 horizontal lines and 2 vertical lines connecting the 3 points of the grid's rows and columns.
  3. Players take turns placing their symbol in 1 of the 9 squares of the grid, once per turn. The player represented by X moves first.
  4. Players continue alternatively placing symbols until the winning condition is met, or there are no more moves remaining, which will result in a tie.

Winning condition

The player who places their symbol in a row, column, or diagonal on the grid first wins the game.
